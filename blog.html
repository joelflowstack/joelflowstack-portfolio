Build logThree.js

Why the cube kept freezing mid-animation

A scroll-driven Three.js animation is easiest to get wrong with mutable state. The original version tracked "current angle" and "moves done" across frames — and desynced constantly. The fix was rebuilding it stateless: every frame recomputed fresh from scroll position and elapsed time, with nothing carried over from the previous frame. Once that architecture was in place, an entire category of bugs simply stopped being possible.

Build logMaterials

Getting the checkerboard black right

Three material passes before landing on the final look: glowing purple/cyan labels first, then a metallic teal-glass "circuit panel" direction, and finally the plain glossy obsidian checker that actually matched the reference video. The lesson wasn't really about materials — it was about trusting a concrete visual reference over abstract "make it look techy" instructions.

Build logAnimation

The boot-solve overshoot bug

Pieces are supposed to fly in from scattered positions and settle into a solid cube on page load, with a satisfying spring overshoot. A clamp on the wrong value — the eased output instead of the time fraction feeding into it — flattened that spring and froze pieces mid-flight. Fixed by clamping only the time input, letting the easing function's natural overshoot render, and adding a hard snap-to-exact-position once each piece's timer completes.

Engineering Flow V3 for sub-second response times

Early infrastructure choices meant Flow sometimes took several seconds to respond — long enough to lose someone's attention entirely. Rebuilding the inference layer around low-latency LPU hardware, with automatic multi-provider failover behind it, cut that to near-instant and changed how the bot actually feels to use.
