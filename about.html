About

One builder, full stack of the weird stuff.

Joel Flowstack is a solo digital studio — no agency layer, no account managers, just the person building your project, from the first sketch to the deployed commit.

The approach

Direct, iterative, shipped in the open.

Projects move through GitHub and Vercel — every change is a commit, every commit is deployed. You see progress as it happens, not at the end of a sprint review nobody asked for.

The studio's own site is the proof: the Rubik's cube you scrolled through on the way in was built the same way every client project is — piece by piece, tested in production, corrected in public. Nothing here is a mockup pretending to be a product.

The range

Where 3D web meets AI infrastructure.

Most studios pick a lane: either visual (3D, motion, design) or technical (bots, APIs, automation). This one sits across both, because the most interesting projects usually need them together — an interface that looks alive, backed by something that actually thinks.

That range comes from working solo. There's no visual designer waiting on a backend engineer, no handoff meetings — just one continuous thread from idea to deployed URL.

Working principles

What stays consistent across every project.

Ship something real, fast

A working, deployed version beats a polished mockup. Momentum matters more than a perfect first draft — the details get sharpened once something's live and testable.

No unnecessary process

No sprint ceremonies, no status decks. Just direct communication and visible progress, because that's what actually moves a project forward.

Every build demos itself

If a chatbot can't answer a question about its own capabilities, or a 3D site doesn't showcase what 3D web can do, it isn't finished — it's a placeholder.

Support doesn't end at launch

Sites and bots are living things. Copy changes, integrations break, new ideas come up — ongoing support is part of the deal, not an upsell.

Also building
